I'm sitting in a homely Jacobean-manor-turned-hotel, covered in layers of green vines and circled by centenarian trees, on a rare weekend away. Everything is warm; the late afternoon sun, the mustard armchairs, the dark wooden panels along the walls. I'm three teas deep and have a few hours of quiet to think for the first time in many months.

Over the spring my baby became a toddler, my family bought and moved into our first real home after crappy-rental-hopping for years, and I stopped feeling quite so green at [GitHub Next]().

For the past six months on the Next team, I've been building Ace, a new research prototype focused on enabling real-time, mutiplayer agentic coding. I did a whole tour of it in [this talk]() at AI Engineer London.

(pic of ace)

Ace feels compelling to me beause its not trying to run the same race as the other coding agents. It has all the knobs and buttons as other coding agents, but we're more interested in enabling the work that happens before any code edits are made: what should we build? How to build it? Who needs to be looped in? What's the plan? Do we have the right conext?

As a little side quest, I rebuilt the Next website in Astro and digital-garden-ified it.

(pic of the new site)

The conference talk at AI Engineer. GitHub having a hard time, and being the punching bag for it.

My husband and I both work full-time, so with a small child and a new house, one that is in fact a very old house, with broken doorknobs, peeling wallpaper, jammed windows, and rising damp that needs ro be fixed, we actuely feel the lack of time these days. It's a stretch to meet everyone's basic needs and keep the dish pile from rising too high. I end up thinking a lot about people married to housewives and SAHMs. And the 1971 classic essay [I Want a Wife](https://www.sevanoland.com/uploads/1/1/8/0/118081022/_brady_i_want_a_wife.pdf) by Judy Brady. An excerpt:

> I want a wife who will keep my house clean. A wife who will pick up after my children, a wife who will pick up after me. I want a wife who will keep my clothes clean, ironed, mended, replaced when need be, and who will see to it that my personal things are kept in their proper place so that I can find what I need the minute I need it. I want a wife who cooks the meals, a wife who is a good cook. I want a wife who will plan the menus, do the necessary grocery shopping, prepare the meals, serve them pleasantly, and then do the cleaning up while I do my studying.

Turns out my husband and I would also like a wife. But not in the polyamorous sense. It's 50 years later, but there are still few good solutions here. Except to eat lots of toast and cereal for “meals,” and live in a montessori-themed construction site.
